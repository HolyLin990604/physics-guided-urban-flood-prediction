## 6. Main text versus supplementary recommendation

The main manuscript should carry the minimum figure and table set needed to support the argument from prediction to reliability-aware warning interpretation. Recommended main-text items are the study/workflow figure, the model and reliability-layer schematic, one representative base prediction-performance example, one compact Phase 12 reliability diagnosis figure, one Phase 13 representative failure figure, the Phase 14 confidence-margin figure, Phase 15 scenario category counts or quantitative table, the Phase 15 risk component heatmap, one pixel-level risk or warning map, and the Phase 16 warning action or applicability-boundary summary.

Supplementary materials should carry diagnostic breadth: additional Phase 12 timestep, seed, depth-bias, boundary, and scenario plots; additional Phase 13 repeated failure-case maps; Phase 14 cross-seed disagreement details; detailed Phase 15 risk score distributions and repeated false-dry pixel maps; and Phase 16 warning count, pixel warning, and high-risk distribution plots if they cannot fit in the main text.

Main text claims should use exact quantitative facts only where they are already established in the cited phase documents or generated CSV/JSON outputs. Supplementary materials can provide the supporting diagnostic detail without turning the main Results section into a catalog of all generated outputs.
